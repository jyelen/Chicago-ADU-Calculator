2 — Project basics & site massing
Massing & ordinance limits (SO2024-0008918, eff. April 1, 2026)

Second ADU — 60% AMI affordability requirement applies

With 2+ ADUs added, 50% of units (rounded down) must be rented at the city-set 60% AMI rate. For 2 ADUs this means 1 unit must be affordable. This covenant runs with the land for 30 years and is recorded with the Cook County Recorder of Deeds. A $500 registration fee applies (§2-44-106).

3 — Financing
Paying cash avoids interest and maximizes day-one cash flow. Best for owners with substantial savings and a low-rate existing mortgage they don't want to disturb. No monthly debt service.
A cash-out refi replaces your entire existing mortgage at the new rate. If your current rate is below today's market, you'll pay the higher rate on your full outstanding balance — not just the ADU portion. The cash-out amount is capped at total project cost.
Construction loans are short-term (12–18 months), interest-only during build, then convert to permanent financing. Rates carry a risk premium. ADU-specific lending products are limited in Chicago. Loan amount is capped at total project cost.
$0 (no grant) $10k planning + $75k construction (NHS max)
NHS Chicago offered up to $10k (planning grant) + $75k (construction, as a 5-year forgivable loan) for income-eligible households — a maximum of $85k total. Program is currently paused — check nhschicago.org for updates. The slider above reflects any grant or subsidy amount; use $0 for no assistance, up to $85k for the NHS maximum, or any value in between to model a partial award or alternative program. Cook County homeowners may also qualify for a Home Improvement Exemption deferring property tax increases for up to 4 years (applied automatically by the Assessor's Office from building permits).
4 — Operating assumptions
Insurance: Standard homeowner policies typically do not cover ADU rentals — a separate landlord policy is needed. Property tax: Adding an ADU may raise assessed value; property classification may also change (e.g. single-family → two-flat). Home Improvement Exemption can defer the increase for up to 4 years. Short-term rentals prohibited: ADUs permitted under this ordinance may not be used for Airbnb/VRBO — minimum lease terms apply. Fine: $1,500–$3,000/offense (§17-9-0201-F(17)).

AMI & rent limit methodology

Chicago-Naperville-Joliet Metro 2025 AMI (4-person HH) ≈ $115,700. HUD household-size adjustment factors: 1-person = 70% ($80,990), 2-person = 80% ($92,560), 3-person = 90% ($104,130), 4-person = 100% ($115,700). Affordable rent is 30% of monthly income at the relevant AMI × household size. The tool maps bedroom count to expected household size: studio → 1-person, 1BR → 2-person, 2BR → 3-person, 3BR → 4-person.

Chicago DOH 2025 published rent limits (from the city's Income & Rent Limits chart) differ slightly from the 30%-of-income calculation because DOH uses 1.5 occupants/bedroom and specific utility allowances. For the most precise affordability compliance figures, use the city's published tables: 2025 Income and Rent Limits (PDF).
