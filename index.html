Second ADU — affordability mandate applies

With 2+ ADUs, 50% of units (rounded down) must be rented at the city-set 60% AMI rate. For 2 ADUs, 1 unit must meet this requirement. This runs with the land and is recorded with the Cook County Recorder of Deeds for 30 years. A $500 registration fee applies. §2-44-106(d)–(i)

3 — Financing
Paying cash avoids interest entirely and maximizes rental cash flow from day one. Best for homeowners with substantial savings and a low-rate existing mortgage they don't want to disturb. Opportunity cost (the return forgone on that capital) is typically estimated at 4–7% annually and is not reflected in the cash-flow figures shown below.
HELOCs use your home's equity as collateral. Lenders typically cap combined LTV at 80–85%. ADU projects are tricky: the loan must be sized on pre-construction equity since the ADU's added value won't be appraised until after completion. Variable rates are common. NHS Chicago ADU resources →
A cash-out refi replaces your entire existing mortgage at the new rate — so if your current rate is below today's market, you'll pay the higher rate on your full outstanding balance. The "out-of-pocket cash needed" figure below reflects only the gap between your loan amount and total project cost.
Construction loans are short-term (12–18 months), interest-only during build, then convert to permanent financing. Rates carry a risk premium. ADU-specific lending products are limited in Chicago — few lenders have standardized ADU underwriting as of 2026.
NHS Chicago / City grant
NHS Chicago offered up to $10k (planning) + $75k (construction, as a 5-year forgivable loan) for income-eligible households. Program is currently paused — check nhschicago.org for updates. Cook County homeowners may qualify for a Home Improvement Exemption to offset property tax increases for up to 4 years (Cook County Assessor's Office applies this automatically from building permits).
4 — Operating assumptions
Insurance: Standard homeowner policies do not typically cover ADU rentals. A separate landlord/dwelling policy is needed — an evolving product area for Illinois ADUs. Property tax: Adding an ADU may increase assessed value. The Home Improvement Exemption can defer increases for up to 4 years. Property classification may also change (e.g., from single-family to two-flat), affecting your tax tier. Vacancy: Chicago pilot ADUs in desirable neighborhoods reported strong occupancy; 5–8% vacancy is a reasonable baseline assumption.

Rent data & AMI methodology
HUD FY2025 Small Area Fair Market Rents (SAFMR)

When a user enters a Chicago ZIP code, the tool loads HUD's FY2025 Small Area Fair Market Rent (SAFMR) data for that ZIP code. SAFMRs are published annually by the U.S. Department of Housing and Urban Development. They represent the 40th percentile of gross rents (including utilities) for standard-quality, recently-rented units — excluding subsidized units and units rented more than 2 years ago. Chicago-Naperville-Joliet Metro (METRO16980M16980) is a mandatory SAFMR area, meaning Cook County PHAs are required to use ZIP-level data rather than a single countywide figure.

FMRs are a market data benchmark, not a subsidy or a price cap. They are used here to: (1) give users a publicly-verified rent reference point by ZIP code and bedroom count; (2) anchor the affordability calculations. Market rents in some neighborhoods will be above the FMR; in others, below. Users can and should adjust the rent slider to reflect their own research (Zillow, Craigslist, Redfin, neighborhood knowledge).

Data source: HUD SAFMR dataset, FY2025 (effective October 2024). Cook County ZIP-level data accessed via USHousingData.com. Data is baked into the tool for offline use; update annually from the HUD SAFMR Excel file at HUD User.
AMI calculations

Area Median Income (AMI) values use the Chicago-Naperville-Joliet HUD Metro FMR Area. The FY2025 AMI for a 4-person household is approximately $115,700. Household-size adjustments follow HUD methodology (Section 8 income limit factors): 1-person = 70%, 2-person = 80%, 3-person = 90%, 4-person = 100%. The tool adjusts the displayed household size to match the unit type — studios use 1-person assumptions; 1-bedroom units use 2-person; 2-bedroom units use 3-person; 3-bedroom units use 4-person. Affordable rent is calculated as 30% of monthly gross income at the relevant AMI level and household size.

The 60% AMI rent cap used in the two-ADU affordability mandate is published annually by the City of Chicago Department of Housing. Current 2025 rent limits are available at chicago.gov/content/dam/city/sites/affordable-requirements-ordinance.
