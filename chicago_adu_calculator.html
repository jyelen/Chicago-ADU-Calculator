2 — Project basics
Two-ADU affordability requirement

When a property has 2+ ADUs, Chicago's ordinance requires that 50% of units (rounding down) be rented at a rate affordable to households at 60% AMI. For 2 ADUs, 1 unit must meet this requirement.

3 — Financing
Paying cash avoids interest entirely but ties up capital. Your opportunity cost is the return forgone — typically estimated at 4–7% annually. No monthly debt service maximizes cash flow from day one. Best suited to homeowners with substantial savings and low-rate existing mortgages they don't want to disturb.
HELOCs and home equity loans use your property's equity as collateral. Lenders typically cap combined LTV at 80–85%. ADU projects are tricky: appraisals often don't reflect the ADU's added value until it's built, so the loan may need to be sized on pre-construction equity. Variable rates are common on HELOCs. A cash-out refi replaces your entire existing mortgage at the new rate. If your current rate is below today's market, you'll pay the higher rate on your full balance — not just the ADU portion. Run both scenarios carefully. Works best when your existing rate is near current market rates or when you need a large lump sum.
Construction loans are short-term (12–18 months), interest-only during build, then convert to permanent financing. Rates are higher due to lender risk. ADU-specific lending products are still developing in Chicago — few lenders have standardized ADU underwriting. Expect underwriting scrutiny and limited options compared to standard HELOCs. NHS Chicago grant / City incentive
The NHS Chicago ADU grant offered up to $10k (planning) + $75k (construction) as a 5-year forgivable loan for income-eligible households. Program is currently paused — check nhschicago.org for updates. Cook County homeowners may qualify for a Home Improvement Exemption to offset property tax increases for up to 4 years after construction.
4 — Operating assumptions
Insurance: Standard homeowner policies typically do not cover ADU rentals. You will likely need a separate landlord / dwelling policy — an evolving product area for ADUs in Illinois. Consult an independent broker experienced with rental properties. Rates shown are rough estimates only.

Property tax: Cook County Assessor notes that ADU additions may raise assessed value, similar to other improvements. A Home Improvement Exemption can defer increases for up to 4 years for qualifying owners. Property reclassification (e.g. from single-family to two-flat) is also possible and affects your tax bill differently.
